Abstract

        New laws that have been enacted in the UK and the EU are damaging online privacy due to online age verification with 
        IDs, ways to increase privacy include stronger passwords and VPNs.

Introduction

        With the recent enaction of the Online Safety Act[1] and the furthering along of the EU ‘Chat Control’ proposal,[2] 
        your privacy online is slowly being degraded by using online age verification systems operated by 3rd parties that 
        can be hacked. Recently, a customer service company (5CA) was hacked and over 70000 photos of government IDs of Discord 
        (a messaging app marketed for gamers) users were leaked.[3][4] Unfortunately, there will be more of this to come, which 
        means you will have to take matters into your own hands.

Password

        The easiest, but arduous, method to protect yourself is by having strong passwords. Users go against their interests 
        (security) in exchange for simplicity in a password,[5] this means most users are likely to have a less secure password 
        so they can remember it easier. In 2010 a study was done where most passwords on select websites were only 8 characters long 
        and only used the alphabet.[6] Stronger passwords are normally either a string of random alphanumeric characters or a few 
        words with a dash in between each (if you want an even stronger password you could make your own cipher, switching certain 
        characters for others). This requires the help of a password manager, the ones I found to be good when researching were KeePass 
        and Bitwarden. KeePass is open source and has many builds for all platforms, Bitwarden is a good cloud-based alternative with 
        the option of self-hosting if you choose to do so. This should be combined with two-factor authentication (2FA), this asks for 
        a code that is sent to your email or authenticator app. If your account details for a website get leaked in a data breach, the 
        hacker would need access to your phone or email as well to be able to access your account.

VPN

        A common misconception of virtual private networks (VPNs) are that they make you completely anonymous, what they do is encrypt 
        your network traffic and change your IP address, this means your internet service provider (ISP) can’t access the private data 
        transmitted through the VPN. A VPN can help enhance your privacy online and for example help prevent a man-in-the-middle attack 
        on a public Wi-Fi network. The recommended VPN is Mullvad, in 2023 they were subject to a search warrant from the National Operations 
        Department (NOA) of the Swedish Police and they left without any customer information.[7] You can also pay in cash or with 
        selected cryptocurrencies if you choose to do so.
